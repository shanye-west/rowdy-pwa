# 12v12 Ryder Cup Style Event - In-Round Rules Official Guide

## Status and Scope

This guide governs only decisions that arise during a round, during a hole, or while determining the result of a single match-play hole or match.

This guide does not decide:

- total team match points;
- overall event score;
- event standings;
- pairings;
- lineup selection;
- trophies, prizes, side pools, or skins;
- overall tournament tie-breaks;
- whether an unstarted or completed match counts toward the event total.

If a question is about the overall event rather than how to play a ball, apply a penalty, calculate a hole score, or determine the current match result, the answer is: **outside this guide; ask the Committee.**

This guide applies to these four match-play formats:

| Format | Players | Match scoring | Gross/net |
|---|---:|---|---|
| 2-Man Scramble | 2v2 | One team ball score per side | Gross |
| 2-Man Shamble | 2v2 | Lower partner score per side after selected drive | Gross |
| 2-Man Best Ball / Four-Ball | 2v2 | Lower partner score per side | Net |
| Singles | 1v1 | Individual score vs. opponent | Net |

The 2023 Rules of Golf govern unless this guide or an announced Local Rule expressly modifies the procedure for the format being played.

# 1. Authority and Ruling Hierarchy

## 1.1 Apply authorities in this order

For any in-round decision, apply the following hierarchy:

1. Announced Local Rules, course rules, and in-round Terms of the Competition.
2. This In-Round Rules Official Guide.
3. The 2023 Rules of Golf and Definitions.
4. A Committee or referee ruling under Rule 20 principles.

If this guide is silent, use the Rules of Golf. If the Rules of Golf are silent because the format is a Committee-created format, decide the issue by equity, consistency, and the closest Rules of Golf principle.

## 1.2 Default assumptions unless announced otherwise

Use these defaults unless the Committee has clearly announced something different before the round or before the relevant match begins:

- All four formats are match play.
- Scramble is gross.
- Shamble is gross.
- Best Ball / Four-Ball is net.
- Singles is net.
- Concessions are allowed.
- The general penalty in match play is loss of hole.
- Penalty relief, such as penalty-area relief, stroke-and-distance, and unplayable-ball relief, adds penalty stroke(s) to the player or side score; it is not automatically loss of hole.
- Do not play two balls in match play.
- Format placement off the putting green is one club-length, no nearer the hole, same area and same cut/condition, unless Rules relief changes the legal playing area.
- Format placement on the putting green is one putter-head length, no nearer the hole.
- No minimum selected drives or tee shots apply unless announced.
- No preferred lies, lift-clean-place, rake-and-place, mulligans, or free relief from non-qualifying conditions apply unless announced.

## 1.3 Required facts before giving a ruling

A rules official must identify these facts before ruling:

1. Which format is being played: Scramble, Shamble, Best Ball/Four-Ball, or Singles.
2. Whether the match is gross or net.
3. Which ball matters:
   - Scramble: selected ball or possible selected ball.
   - Shamble: selected drive or own-ball phase ball.
   - Four-Ball: which player's ball.
   - Singles: the player's ball.
4. The area of the course: teeing area, general area, bunker, penalty area, or putting green.
5. The specific condition involved: obstruction, abnormal course condition, penalty area, lost/OB, unplayable, embedded, wrong ball, wrong place, ball moved, concession, order of play, advice, etc.
6. Whether a stroke has already been made after the possible breach or relief decision.
7. Whether either side has started the next hole.
8. In net formats, whether the player receives a handicap stroke on the hole.

## 1.4 Required ruling output

A ruling should be stated in this structure:

1. **Ruling**: the correct decision.
2. **Procedure**: exactly what the player or side must do next.
3. **Penalty**: no penalty, one penalty stroke, general penalty/loss of hole, or disqualification-level issue.
4. **Who is affected**: player only, partner only, side, or both sides.
5. **Hole-score effect**: gross score, net score, side score, or hole result.
6. **Missing fact**, if any: identify the fact needed before a final ruling can be given.

# 2. Core Definitions

## 2.1 Match-play terms

**Side**: A player or partnership competing in the current match. In 2v2 formats, a side has two partners. In singles, a side has one player.

**Partner**: A player on the same side in a 2v2 match.

**Opponent**: A player or side on the other side of the current match.

**Match**: One side against one opposing side over the assigned holes.

**Hole result**: Won, lost, or tied for the current match.

**General penalty**: Loss of hole in match play.

**Penalty stroke**: A stroke added to a player or side score under a Rule, such as penalty-area relief, stroke-and-distance, or unplayable-ball relief.

**Gross score**: Strokes made plus penalty strokes, with no handicap strokes applied.

**Net score**: Gross score adjusted by handicap strokes received on that hole.

## 2.2 Course-area terms

Use the Rules of Golf definitions for the five areas of the course:

1. Teeing area.
2. General area.
3. Bunker.
4. Penalty area.
5. Putting green.

Within the general area, this guide also uses **same cut/condition** for format placement:

- Fairway remains fairway.
- Rough remains rough.
- Fringe/apron remains fringe/apron unless the Committee defines it differently.
- Natural area remains natural area.
- Waste area remains waste area unless the Committee defines it as a bunker.

Same cut/condition applies to scramble/shamble format placement when no separate Rules of Golf relief procedure is being used. Same cut/condition does not override official Rules relief.

## 2.3 Format terms

**Selected ball**: In scramble, the ball whose result the side chooses for the next team stroke.

**Selected spot**: The exact spot where the selected ball came to rest, or the legal playing area established after a Rules relief procedure for that selected ball.

**Selected drive**: In shamble, the valid tee shot chosen by the side as the starting location for both partners' second strokes.

**Selected-drive spot**: The exact spot where the selected drive came to rest, or the legal playing area established after a Rules relief procedure for that selected drive.

**Placement area**: The area where a player or side may place a ball under the format rule.

**Own-ball phase**: The part of a shamble, Four-Ball, or singles hole where each player plays only that player's own ball.

**Valid shot**: A stroke made by a player entitled to play, from a permitted place, using conforming equipment, and not invalidated by the Rules or this guide.

**Non-selected ball**: A ball result not chosen in scramble or during the tee-shot selection phase of shamble. A non-selected ball usually does not affect the side's score, but independent penalties still apply.

## 2.4 Measurement terms

**One club-length for official Rules relief**: Use the Rules of Golf definition.

**One club-length for scramble/shamble format placement**: Use the longest club carried by either partner on that side, other than a putter, unless the Committee announces a different method. A side may not borrow a club from an opponent or another group solely to create a larger placement area.

**One putter-head length**: The length of the putter head only, not the shaft.

**No nearer the hole**: The ball must not be closer to the hole than the original spot or relevant reference point.

# 3. Universal Match-Play Rules

## 3.1 How a hole is won

A side wins a hole when:

- that side completes the hole in fewer strokes than the opposing side under the format's gross/net scoring rule;
- the opponent concedes the hole;
- the opponent gets a general penalty, which is loss of hole; or
- the Rules otherwise decide the hole.

A hole is tied when both sides have the same hole score after applying the format and handicap strokes, or when the sides properly agree the hole is tied after the hole has started.

## 3.2 Concessions

A player may concede an opponent's next stroke, the hole, or the match, unless concessions have been prohibited before the round.

A concession must be clear. It can be verbal or by an action that clearly shows intent.

A concession is final. It cannot be declined or withdrawn.

A conceded next stroke counts as made for the opponent's score.

In team formats, a player whose stroke has been conceded must not continue play of the hole if doing so would help the partner.

## 3.3 Giving number of strokes taken

When an opponent asks for the number of strokes taken on a hole, the player or side must give the correct number, including penalty strokes.

In match play, giving wrong stroke information can result in loss of hole if not corrected in time.

A player must tell the opponent about a penalty as soon as reasonably possible unless the opponent already knows about it.

## 3.4 Order of play

In match play, order matters.

From the teeing area, the side with the honor plays first. After both sides have started a hole, the ball farther from the hole normally plays first.

If a player or side plays out of turn, there is no penalty, but the opponent may cancel the stroke if done promptly and before another stroke is made.

Exception: if sides agree to play out of turn to save time, the right to cancel that stroke is waived.

Within a partner side in Scramble, Shamble, and Four-Ball, partners may play in the order the side considers best, subject to the specific restrictions in this guide.

## 3.5 Do not play two balls in match play

If there is doubt about a procedure in match play, do not play two balls. Instead:

1. State the issue to the opponent.
2. Decide whether to request a ruling.
3. Play on under one procedure.
4. Preserve the right to a later ruling by making the request in time under Rule 20 match-play principles.

## 3.6 Timely ruling requests

A match-play ruling request should be made before either side starts the next hole when the issue concerns a hole just completed.

For the final hole of a match, the request must be made before the match result is final under the Committee's method.

If a side waits too long, an incorrect result may stand even if the ruling would have been different.

## 3.7 Agreement between sides

Sides may agree on facts and may agree on how to resolve uncertainty if they are not knowingly ignoring a Rule or penalty.

Sides may not agree to waive a known Rule or ignore a known penalty. A deliberate agreement to ignore a known Rule is a serious breach.

## 3.8 If opponents give the wrong procedure

An opponent does not become the rules official by insisting on a procedure.

If a side is pressured into a wrong procedure and the issue is raised in time, the Committee should correct the procedure if possible.

If the side makes a stroke from a wrong place and the issue is not raised in time, the wrong-place penalty or loss of hole may still apply.

# 4. Universal Rules of Golf Procedures

## 4.1 Ball lost or out of bounds

A ball is lost if not found within the search time allowed by the Rules.

A lost or out-of-bounds ball requires stroke-and-distance relief unless a Local Rule provides another option.

Stroke-and-distance means the player or side adds one penalty stroke and plays again from where the previous stroke was made.

Format application:

- Scramble: if at least one partner has a valid ball available from that stroke, the side may select that ball and ignore the lost/OB ball. If all partner balls from that stroke are lost or OB, the side proceeds under stroke-and-distance from the previous selected spot.
- Shamble tee shots: if one tee shot is valid, the side may select it and ignore the other lost/OB tee shot. If all tee shots are lost or OB, the side proceeds under stroke-and-distance.
- Shamble own-ball phase, Four-Ball, and Singles: apply the Rules to the individual player's own ball.

## 4.2 Provisional ball

A provisional ball may be played when a ball might be lost outside a penalty area or might be out of bounds.

A provisional ball becomes the ball in play only under the Rules.

Format application:

- Scramble: a provisional may be selected only if it is validly in play.
- Shamble: a provisional/replayed tee ball may become the selected drive only if validly in play.
- Four-Ball/Singles: apply the Rules to the individual player's ball.

## 4.3 Penalty areas

A ball in a penalty area may be played as it lies with no penalty.

If penalty-area relief is taken, add one penalty stroke and use the available relief option under Rule 17.

There is no free relief under abnormal-course-condition rules for a ball in a penalty area unless a specific Rule or Local Rule allows it.

## 4.4 Unplayable ball

A player may treat a ball as unplayable anywhere except in a penalty area.

Unplayable-ball relief adds one penalty stroke.

Use the relief options available under Rule 19.

Format application:

- Scramble: if the selected ball is unplayable, the side adds one penalty stroke and proceeds from the correct relief area for the selected ball.
- Shamble before second strokes: if the selected drive is treated as unplayable, each player who continues from the relief area adds one penalty stroke to that player's individual hole score.
- Shamble own-ball phase, Four-Ball, and Singles: penalty applies to the individual player's ball unless Four-Ball penalty-allocation rules make it affect the partner or side.

## 4.5 Embedded ball

If embedded-ball relief is available in the general area, it is free relief.

Format application:

- Scramble: if the selected ball is embedded and relief is available, establish the correct relief area for that selected ball, then both partners play from the legal relief area under the scramble procedure.
- Shamble before second strokes: if the selected drive is embedded and relief is available, establish the correct relief area for the selected drive, then both partners play second strokes from the legal relief area under the shamble procedure.
- Own-ball formats: the individual player takes relief under the Rules.

## 4.6 Ball moved

If a player's ball at rest is moved, first decide what caused it to move:

- player;
- opponent;
- partner;
- caddie;
- outside influence;
- natural forces.

Then apply the Rules to decide whether the ball is replaced or played as it lies and whether a penalty applies.

If the ball must be replaced and the exact spot is unknown, estimate the spot using reasonable judgment.

## 4.7 Wrong ball

A stroke at a wrong ball is not valid unless a specific exception applies.

In singles match play, playing a wrong ball normally results in loss of hole.

In Four-Ball, the player who plays a wrong ball normally gets the penalty; the partner's score may still count unless the breach helped the partner or hurt an opponent under Rule 23 principles.

In Scramble, a wrong-ball stroke is not a valid selection. If the wrong-ball result is selected or used, the side is treated as having used an invalid/wrong-place result and may lose the hole.

In Shamble, wrong-ball treatment depends on timing:

- Before or at the selected-drive phase, the wrong-ball stroke is not a valid selected drive or second shot.
- After own-ball phase begins, penalize the individual player; the partner's score may still count unless helped.

## 4.8 Wrong place

A ball has been played from a wrong place if it is played from outside the area required by the Rules or format.

In match play, the penalty for playing from a wrong place is usually loss of hole if the breach applies to the player or side whose score is being used.

Scramble and shamble placement outside the allowed area is wrong place.

If a wrong-place shot is not selected and gives no help, it is normally disregarded in Scramble and may only affect that individual player in Shamble/Four-Ball.

If the side selects or uses the wrong-place shot, the side is exposed to loss of hole.

## 4.9 Improving conditions

A player must not improve the conditions affecting the stroke except as allowed by the Rules or by a format rule.

Examples of prohibited improvement include:

- pressing down rough behind the ball;
- moving or bending a growing object to improve swing or line;
- smoothing a bunker before a stroke when not allowed;
- improving the lie after own-ball phase begins.

Format placement allows the player to place in the allowed placement area, but it does not allow improving the course outside what the placement rule permits.

## 4.10 Bunkers

When a ball is in a bunker, bunker restrictions apply unless a format rule or Local Rule expressly changes the procedure.

Scramble and shamble default: if the selected ball/drive is in a bunker, the next ball(s) must be played from that same bunker unless valid penalty relief is taken.

Raking or smoothing before placing is allowed only if announced. If not announced, do not improve bunker conditions beyond what the Rules allow.

## 4.11 Putting green

On the putting green, players may mark, lift, clean, and replace balls as allowed by the Rules.

Damage on the putting green may be repaired as allowed by the Rules.

Scramble/shamble format placement on the putting green is within one putter-head length, no nearer the hole, unless announced otherwise.

## 4.12 Teeing area

Each player must start a hole from the correct teeing area.

In match play, if a player starts from outside the teeing area, there is no penalty, but the opponent may cancel the stroke promptly.

In Scramble/Shamble, an improperly played tee shot is not a valid selection if it is cancelled or otherwise invalid.

# 5. Relief and Format Placement - Controlling Rules

## 5.1 Core principle

Official Rules relief and scramble/shamble format placement are separate procedures.

Use this order:

1. Select the ball or drive to be used, if the format requires selection.
2. Determine whether that selected ball or selected drive is entitled to relief under the Rules of Golf.
3. If relief is taken, determine the correct reference point and legal relief area under the Rules.
4. Then apply the format's play-from-that-area procedure.
5. Do not force a side to use its scramble/shamble format placement before deciding whether Rules relief applies.

## 5.2 Relief first; format placement second

When the selected ball or selected drive has interference from an obstruction, abnormal course condition, embedded-ball condition, animal hole, temporary water, or similar condition, the side first decides whether Rules relief is available and whether to take it.

The legal relief procedure is based on the original selected ball/drive position, not on where the opponents think the side should place under the scramble or shamble format.

Opponents may not require the side to use the format one-club placement first and then claim relief is no longer needed.

## 5.3 Same cut/condition does not override Rules relief

The same cut/condition limitation applies only to normal format placement when no separate Rules relief is being taken.

If the correct Rules relief area for a selected ball in the rough is in the fairway, the side may use that fairway relief area.

If the correct Rules relief area for a selected ball in the fairway is in the rough, the side must use the legal relief area even though it is worse.

The Rules relief area controls.

## 5.4 No automatic double club-length

The one club-length used for official Rules relief belongs to the Rules relief procedure.

The one club-length used for scramble/shamble format placement belongs to the format procedure.

When Rules relief is taken from a selected ball/drive, the side must play from the legal relief area. The side does not automatically get an additional full club-length outside the legal relief area unless the Committee has expressly adopted that special event rule.

Default in this guide:

- The side first establishes the legal relief area under the Rules.
- Each partner then places or plays from within that legal relief area as allowed by the format.
- The ball must not be played from outside the legal relief area.

## 5.5 Obstruction example - controlling ruling

Facts: In 2-Man Scramble, Team A selects a ball lying in rough. The ball has interference from an immovable obstruction, such as a cart path. The nearest point of complete relief and legal relief area are in fairway.

Correct ruling:

1. Team A selected the ball in the rough.
2. The selected ball has obstruction interference.
3. Team A may take free relief under the Rules from the original selected ball position.
4. If the proper relief area is in fairway, Team A may use the fairway relief area.
5. Team A's partners then play from the legal relief area under the scramble procedure.
6. Team B may not require Team A to take the normal scramble one-club rough placement first and then deny obstruction relief because that placed ball no longer has interference.

Penalty: none if Team A follows the correct relief procedure. If Team A instead plays from a wrong place and the issue is not corrected in time, the side may lose the hole.

## 5.6 Normal format placement without relief

If no Rules relief is being taken, the normal scramble/shamble placement restrictions apply:

- one club-length off the putting green;
- one putter-head length on the putting green;
- no nearer the hole;
- same area of the course;
- same cut/condition in the general area.

Examples:

- Selected ball in rough and one club-length reaches fairway: stay in rough.
- Selected drive in fairway and one club-length reaches rough: stay in fairway unless the player chooses another fairway point.
- Selected ball in bunker: stay in same bunker unless penalty relief is taken.
- Selected ball in penalty area and played as it lies: stay in same penalty area.

# 6. 2-Man Scramble - Gross Match Play

## 6.1 Format summary

Both partners tee off. The side selects one valid ball. Both partners play from the selected spot. The side repeats this process until one valid team ball is holed or the hole is otherwise completed.

The side has one gross score on the hole.

No handicap strokes are applied.

## 6.2 What counts as the side's score

The side's score is the number of team strokes in the selected path plus penalty strokes that apply to the side or selected path.

When both partners hit from the same selected spot, those attempts collectively count as one team stroke once a valid result is selected.

Example: both partners hit second shots and the side selects Player B's second shot. The side has made one second team stroke, not two.

## 6.3 Starting the hole

Both partners tee off from their assigned teeing area.

If one partner's tee shot is invalid, cancelled, lost, OB, or otherwise unavailable, the side may select the other partner's valid tee shot.

If both tee shots are lost or OB, the side proceeds under stroke-and-distance from the teeing area and is playing its third team stroke from the teeing area.

## 6.4 Selecting a ball

A side may select any valid ball played by either partner.

A ball that is lost, out of bounds, not found within search time, or not validly in play cannot be selected.

A ball in a penalty area may be selected if its location is known or virtually certain. The side may either play from the penalty area or take penalty relief.

Once either partner makes a stroke from the selected spot or selected relief area, the selection is locked unless correction is required by the Rules.

## 6.5 Marking and lifting

Mark the selected spot before lifting the selected ball.

If the selected ball is lifted before marking, apply the Rules of Golf unless the Committee announced a no-penalty convention.

After the selected spot is marked, non-selected balls may be lifted.

A lifted ball may be cleaned when lifted under the scramble format unless a Local Rule prohibits cleaning.

## 6.6 Normal placement when no Rules relief is taken

Off the putting green, each partner places a ball within one club-length of the selected spot, no nearer the hole, in the same area and same cut/condition.

On the putting green, each partner places a ball within one putter-head length of the selected spot, no nearer the hole.

The side may choose partner order.

## 6.7 General area placement

Fairway selected spot: place in fairway-height grass only.

Rough selected spot: place in rough only.

Fringe/apron selected spot: remain on fringe/apron unless the Committee defines it otherwise.

Natural/waste area selected spot: remain in the same condition unless the Committee defines it differently.

## 6.8 Scramble relief procedure

If the selected ball is entitled to Rules relief:

1. Identify the selected ball.
2. Mark or otherwise establish the original selected spot.
3. Determine the applicable Rule.
4. Determine whether relief is free or penalty relief.
5. Establish the correct reference point, relief area, and limits.
6. If penalty relief applies, add the penalty stroke(s) to the side score.
7. Both partners then play from the legal relief area under the scramble procedure.

Do not apply normal same-cut format placement before the relief decision.

Do not require the side to remain in the original cut if the correct Rules relief area is in another cut.

## 6.9 Scramble bunkers

If the selected ball is in a bunker, both partners must play from that same bunker unless the side takes valid penalty relief that allows or requires leaving the bunker.

Default placement is within one club-length, no nearer the hole, in the same bunker.

Raking or smoothing before placement is allowed only if announced. If not announced, do not improve the bunker beyond what the Rules allow.

A ball placed outside the bunker when the selected spot was in the bunker is played from a wrong place unless valid relief was taken.

## 6.10 Scramble penalty areas

If the selected ball is in a penalty area and the side plays it as it lies, both partners must play from within the same penalty area within one club-length, no nearer the hole.

If the side takes penalty-area relief from the selected ball, add one penalty stroke to the side's score and proceed under the appropriate Rule 17 relief option.

A non-selected ball in a penalty area is ignored unless an independent penalty applies.

## 6.11 Scramble unplayable ball

If the selected ball is outside a penalty area and the side declares it unplayable, add one penalty stroke to the side's score and use an available unplayable-ball relief option.

Both partners then play from the legal relief area.

A non-selected unplayable ball is ignored.

## 6.12 Scramble putting green

On the putting green, mark the selected ball.

Each partner places within one putter-head length, no nearer the hole.

The side may choose putting order.

Once any valid ball is holed, the side's score for the hole is fixed. Later putts from the same spot do not count and may be treated as practice only if allowed.

## 6.13 Scramble partner standing behind

Scramble-specific default: a partner may stand behind or near the line to observe a teammate's stroke and gather information for the side, provided the partner does not physically assist, distract, interfere, cause unreasonable delay, or violate an announced restriction.

This scramble allowance does not let a caddie align a player in violation of Rule 10.

## 6.14 Scramble penalties and invalid shots

A non-selected poor result is normally ignored.

A non-selected independent breach still applies if the Rule attaches the penalty regardless of whether the shot is selected. Examples include non-conforming equipment, serious misconduct, improper practice, advice to an opponent, and deliberate interference.

A shot from outside the allowed placement area is not a valid selection.

If the side selects and continues with a wrong-place shot, the side may lose the hole.

# 7. 2-Man Shamble - Gross Match Play

## 7.1 Format summary

Both partners tee off. The side selects one valid tee shot as the selected drive. Both partners play their second strokes from the selected-drive spot or legal selected-drive relief area. After each partner plays a second stroke, each partner plays that player's own ball for the rest of the hole.

The side's score is the lower gross score of the two partners unless the Committee has announced aggregate or another scoring method.

No handicap strokes are applied.

## 7.2 Difference from scramble

In Scramble, the side keeps selecting one ball after every stroke until holed.

In Shamble, the side selects only the tee shot. After both partners play from the selected-drive area, selection ends and own-ball play begins.

After own-ball phase begins, no more one-club-length shamble placement is allowed unless another Rule gives relief.

## 7.3 Selecting the drive

A side may select either partner's valid tee shot.

A lost or out-of-bounds tee shot cannot be selected unless a provisional or replayed ball has validly become the ball in play.

A tee shot in a penalty area may be selected if the ball's location is known or virtually certain.

Once either partner makes a second stroke from the selected-drive area, the selected drive is locked unless correction is required by the Rules.

## 7.4 Placement for second strokes when no Rules relief is taken

Off the putting green, each partner places a ball within one club-length of the selected-drive spot, no nearer the hole, in the same area and same cut/condition.

On the putting green, each partner places a ball within one putter-head length, no nearer the hole.

This placement applies only to the second stroke.

## 7.5 Selected-drive player's ball and substitution

Default: the player whose drive is selected may lift, clean, and place that same ball within the allowed placement area.

The partner whose drive is not selected may lift that tee ball and place any conforming ball within the allowed placement area unless a one-ball condition or no-substitution rule has been announced.

After each player makes a second stroke, that player's ball is in own-ball phase.

## 7.6 Shamble relief procedure before second strokes

If the selected drive is entitled to Rules relief before second strokes:

1. Identify the selected drive.
2. Mark or otherwise establish the original selected-drive spot.
3. Determine the applicable relief Rule.
4. Determine whether relief is free or penalty relief.
5. Establish the correct reference point, relief area, and limits.
6. Each partner who continues from the relief area plays from that legal relief area.
7. If penalty relief applies, each continuing player's individual score includes the penalty stroke(s).

Do not apply same-cut shamble placement before deciding Rules relief.

If the correct relief area is in fairway even though the selected drive lay in rough, the players may use the fairway relief area.

## 7.7 Shamble bunkers

If the selected drive lies in a bunker, both players must play second strokes from that same bunker unless penalty relief is taken.

Default: place within one club-length, no nearer the hole, in the same bunker.

Raking or smoothing before placement is allowed only if announced.

## 7.8 Shamble penalty areas

If the selected drive lies in a penalty area and the side plays it as it lies, both players place and play from within the same penalty area, within one club-length and no nearer the hole.

If the side takes penalty-area relief from the selected drive, each player who continues from the relief area adds one penalty stroke to that player's individual hole score.

If one player's tee shot is in a penalty area but is not selected, that result is ignored unless an independent penalty applies.

## 7.9 All tee shots lost or out of bounds

If both tee shots are lost or out of bounds, the side has no selected drive in play.

The side must proceed under stroke-and-distance.

A replayed or provisional tee shot becomes a candidate selected drive only when validly in play.

If a replayed/provisional location is selected after both original tee shots are unavailable, each continuing player is lying 3 before making the next stroke from that selected location.

## 7.10 Own-ball phase

Own-ball phase begins for a player as soon as that player makes a second stroke from the selected-drive area.

After own-ball phase begins:

- the player plays only that player's own ball;
- no further shamble placement is allowed;
- relief applies to the individual player's ball;
- a player may not switch to the partner's ball;
- the side's score is the lower gross score of the two partners unless another method was announced.

## 7.11 Four-Ball principles after the selected drive

After the selected drive, shamble uses Four-Ball principles for partner conduct and scoring:

- one or both partners may represent the side;
- one partner's valid score may count even if the other partner picks up or has no valid score;
- partners may play in the order the side considers best;
- partners may give advice to each other;
- a partner's action concerning the other partner's ball may penalize the other partner;
- penalties normally apply only to the player unless the breach helps the partner or hurts an opponent.

## 7.12 Partner standing behind in shamble

Before the second strokes from the selected-drive area, use the shamble selection/placement procedure.

After own-ball phase begins, a partner must not stand on or close to an extension of the line of play behind the ball while the partner makes a stroke to gain information for the player's next stroke.

Penalty: general penalty under Four-Ball principles.

## 7.13 Shamble pickup policy

In best-ball shamble match play, a player whose score cannot affect the side's result on the hole may pick up.

If aggregate scoring, individual prizes, or a special hole-out requirement was announced, the player may need to complete the hole.

A picked-up player has no valid individual score for that hole, but the partner's score may still count.

# 8. 2-Man Best Ball / Four-Ball - Net Match Play

## 8.1 Format summary

Each player plays that player's own ball from the teeing area until holed, conceded, picked up when the score cannot count, or otherwise completed under the Rules.

The side's score for the hole is the lower net score of the two partners.

## 8.2 Net scoring

Each player's gross score is adjusted by that player's handicap strokes on the hole.

The lower net score between the two partners is the side's score for the hole.

The side with the lower net side score wins the hole.

If both sides have the same lower net score, the hole is tied.

## 8.3 Handicap allowance for Four-Ball match play

Default: Four-Ball match play uses 90% of the difference from the lowest Course Handicap in the match, unless the Committee publishes a different handicap method.

Procedure:

1. Determine each player's Course Handicap for the tee played.
2. Identify the lowest Course Handicap among the four players.
3. That lowest player plays at zero.
4. Each other player receives 90% of the difference between that player's Course Handicap and the lowest Course Handicap.
5. Round according to the Committee's handicap/scoring method.
6. Allocate strokes by stroke index.

If the scoring app or Committee publishes hole-by-hole strokes, use the published strokes during the round.

## 8.4 One or both partners may represent the side

A side may have a valid score if only one partner completes the hole.

If one partner has a valid score, the other partner does not need to hole out if that player's score cannot count.

If neither partner has a valid score, the side loses the hole unless the opposing side already conceded or otherwise lost the hole.

## 8.5 Partner order of play

Partners may play in the order the side considers best.

When it is a side's turn in match play, either partner may play.

This does not remove the opponent's normal match-play rights if the wrong side plays out of turn.

## 8.6 Partner advice and actions

Partners may give advice to each other.

A partner may help in ways a caddie may help.

A partner may mark, lift, replace, drop, or place the partner's ball only when the Rules allow that action and the partner would be allowed to do it.

If a player takes an action concerning the partner's ball that would breach a Rule if done by the partner, the partner is treated as breaching the Rule.

## 8.7 Standing behind partner

A player must not stand on or close to an extension of the line of play behind the ball while the partner makes a stroke to gain information for the player's next stroke.

Penalty: general penalty.

## 8.8 Conceded stroke in Four-Ball

If a player's next stroke is conceded, that player has completed the hole with that conceded stroke included.

A player whose stroke was conceded must not continue the hole if doing so would help the partner.

If the player continues anyway, that player's score stands without penalty, but the partner's score cannot count for the side on that hole.

## 8.9 Penalties in Four-Ball

Default: a penalty applies only to the player who breached the Rule.

If the penalty is a one-stroke penalty, add it only to that player's score.

If the penalty is a general penalty/loss of hole, that player has no score that can count for the side on that hole, but the partner may continue and the partner's score may count.

A partner is also penalized when:

- the breach is a 14-club/shared-club breach that applies to the side;
- the player's breach helps the partner's play;
- in match play, the player's breach hurts an opponent's play.

Wrong ball exception: if a player makes a stroke at a wrong ball, only that player gets the wrong-ball penalty; it is not automatically treated as helping the partner or hurting the opponent.

## 8.10 Wrong ball in Four-Ball

If one partner plays a wrong ball, penalize that player.

The partner's score may still count unless the breach helped the partner or hurt an opponent under Rule 23 principles.

The player who played the wrong ball has no score that can count for the side on that hole if the penalty is loss of hole.

## 8.11 Pickup policy

A player may pick up when that player's score cannot count for the side.

Before picking up, confirm that the partner has or can still produce a valid score that may count.

In net play, remember to apply handicap strokes before deciding that a score cannot count.

# 9. Singles - Net Match Play

## 9.1 Format summary

Each player plays one ball under the Rules of Golf.

The lower net score wins the hole.

## 9.2 Handicap allowance for singles match play

Default: singles match play uses 100% of Course Handicap unless the Committee publishes a different method.

Procedure:

1. Determine each player's Course Handicap for the tee played.
2. The lower Course Handicap plays at zero.
3. The higher Course Handicap player receives the difference.
4. Allocate strokes by stroke index.

Example: Player A is 6 and Player B is 14. Player A plays at zero. Player B receives 8 strokes on stroke-index holes 1 through 8.

## 9.3 Plus handicaps

If a plus-handicap player is involved, preserve the plus value and net off the lowest player according to the Committee's handicap method.

Do not convert a plus handicap to zero unless the Committee has announced that method.

## 9.4 Concessions

A player may concede the opponent's next stroke, the hole, or the match.

A conceded stroke counts as made.

A concession is final.

## 9.5 Wrong information

If a player gives the wrong number of strokes taken or fails to tell the opponent about a penalty, apply match-play wrong-information rules.

If corrected in time, there may be no penalty.

If not corrected in time and the wrong information affected the opponent's understanding or play, the player may lose the hole.

## 9.6 Playing out of turn

If a player plays when the opponent should have played, there is no penalty, but the opponent may cancel the stroke promptly.

If cancelled, the player must replay when it is that player's turn.

If not cancelled promptly, the stroke counts.

# 10. Net Handicap Procedures for In-Round Decisions

## 10.1 Use published strokes first

If the Committee or scoring app publishes each player's strokes for each hole, use those published strokes during the round.

The rules official should not recalculate handicaps during the round unless the published strokes are missing, disputed, or clearly wrong.

## 10.2 Stroke index

Net strokes are applied by the course stroke index unless the Committee announces a different allocation.

For a player receiving N strokes:

- If N is 1-18, the player receives one stroke on stroke-index holes 1 through N.
- If N exceeds 18, the player receives one stroke on every hole and a second stroke on stroke-index holes 1 through N minus 18.
- If the player gives strokes due to plus handicap treatment, apply the Committee's published method.

## 10.3 Four-Ball net method

Use Four-Ball match-play handicap strokes for each individual player, then compare the lower net partner scores on the hole.

Do not combine partner handicaps into one side handicap unless the Committee has announced that method.

## 10.4 Singles net method

Use the difference between the two players' handicaps, with the lower player at zero.

Apply strokes hole-by-hole.

## 10.5 Gross rounds override generic scramble/shamble handicap allowances

Scramble and Shamble are gross for this event.

Do not apply generic scramble allowances such as 35%/15%.

Do not apply generic shamble allowances such as 75%.

Those allowances may be useful in other events but are overridden here because these two rounds are gross.

## 10.6 Handicap dispute during a hole

If players disagree about whether a stroke is received on the hole:

1. Check the published match card or scoring app.
2. If unavailable, use the announced handicap method and stroke index.
3. If still unresolved, play the hole under the most reasonable published/known method and request a Committee ruling before the match result is final.

# 11. Advice, Captains, Teammates, and Spectators

## 11.1 Partners

Partners may give each other advice in Scramble, Shamble, and Four-Ball.

Partners may discuss strategy, club selection, target, line, wind, relief options, score situation, and whether to concede.

Advice between opponents is not allowed unless it is public information or otherwise allowed by the Rules.

## 11.2 Team captains and other teammates

A team captain, teammate, or other person may give advice during a round only if the Committee has designated that person as an advice giver under the Rules and announced the scope of that role.

If no advice-giver role was announced, other teammates and captains are spectators for advice purposes and must not give advice to players during a round.

A captain or teammate may give public information, such as the location of the flagstick, distance from a sprinkler head, or the match score, if that information is not advice.

## 11.3 Spectators

Spectators must not give advice.

A spectator may help search for a ball, identify a ball, move out of the way, or provide public information.

If a spectator gives advice, determine whether the player requested it, accepted it, or acted on it.

## 11.4 Caddies

Caddies are governed by Rule 10.

A caddie may give advice to the player they are caddying for and may take actions allowed by the Rules.

A caddie may not align a player in a prohibited way or stand in a restricted position in violation of Rule 10.2b.

# 12. Score, Pickup, and Completion of a Hole

## 12.1 Scramble completion

A scramble side completes the hole when one valid team ball is holed, the hole is conceded, or the hole result is decided.

Once one valid team ball is holed, the side score is fixed.

## 12.2 Shamble completion

A shamble side completes the hole when:

- both partners have holed out or had strokes conceded;
- one partner has a valid score and the other partner picks up because that score cannot count;
- the hole is conceded; or
- the hole result is decided.

The side score is the lower gross partner score unless another in-round scoring method was announced.

## 12.3 Four-Ball completion

A Four-Ball side completes the hole when:

- both partners have completed the hole;
- one partner has completed the hole and the other partner chooses not to or cannot improve the side score;
- the result of the hole is decided; or
- the hole is conceded.

The side score is the lower net partner score.

## 12.4 Singles completion

A singles player completes the hole when the player holes out, has the next stroke conceded, or the result of the hole is decided.

## 12.5 Picking up

A player or partner may pick up when that player's score cannot affect the side's result, unless a Committee rule requires holing out.

In net formats, apply handicap strokes before deciding whether the player's score can still count.

# 13. Player Availability During a Round

## 13.1 One-player side in Scramble

If a scramble side has only one player present, the lone player plays one ball only.

The lone player does not receive a second attempt unless the Committee has announced a one-player scramble exception.

## 13.2 One-player side in Shamble

If a shamble side has only one player present, the lone player plays one tee shot and then continues own-ball.

The lone player does not receive a second tee attempt unless the Committee has announced otherwise.

## 13.3 One-player side in Four-Ball

A Four-Ball side may be represented by one partner.

If an absent partner arrives, that partner may start play only between holes, not during a hole already started.

## 13.4 Late player in Singles

A late singles player is governed by the starting-time and match-play rules set by the Committee.

If a singles match has already started or a hole has been conceded due to absence, do not invent a remedy. Ask the Committee.

# 14. Equipment and Balls

## 14.1 Clubs

Each player must comply with the 14-club rule unless a specific partner-sharing rule applies.

In Four-Ball, partners may share clubs only if they have no more than 14 clubs together.

For scramble and shamble, partners may share clubs only if allowed by the Rules or announced terms. If not sure, do not share clubs.

A player may not borrow an opponent's club to measure a placement or relief area.

## 14.2 Balls

A player must use a conforming ball.

Ball substitution is allowed when a Rule allows it.

Scramble and shamble selected-spot placement allows substitution by default unless a one-ball condition or no-substitution rule is announced.

After shamble own-ball phase begins, after Four-Ball begins, and in Singles, the player must continue with that player's ball unless the Rules allow substitution.

## 14.3 Distance-measuring devices

Distance-measuring devices are allowed unless prohibited by Local Rule.

Do not use a device feature that measures elevation change, recommends club selection, or otherwise provides prohibited assistance unless the Committee has expressly allowed it and the Rules permit it.

# 15. Pace of Play

## 15.1 General standard

Players should play without unreasonable delay.

A player may briefly delay to seek a ruling, help an injured player, or handle another good reason.

## 15.2 Search time

Use the Rules of Golf search time for a potentially lost ball.

After search time expires, the ball is lost even if found later, unless the Rules provide otherwise.

## 15.3 Scramble pace

Decide the selected ball promptly.

Pick up non-selected balls promptly.

On the putting green, decide whether both partners will putt before the first player holes out.

## 15.4 Shamble and Four-Ball pace

A player whose score cannot count should pick up unless the Committee requires holing out.

In net play, confirm handicap strokes before assuming the score cannot count.

## 15.5 Pace penalties

If the Committee has announced a pace policy, apply that policy.

If not, use Rule 5.6 unreasonable-delay principles.

# 16. Edge-Case Rulings

## 16.1 Scramble: selected ball in rough, one club-length reaches fairway

Ruling: stay in rough. Without separate Rules relief, the format placement must remain in the same cut/condition.

## 16.2 Scramble: selected ball in rough with cart-path interference, relief area in fairway

Ruling: relief first. The side may take free relief from the selected ball's original position. If the proper relief area is in fairway, the side may play from that fairway relief area. Opponents may not force the side to use scramble placement in the rough first.

## 16.3 Scramble: selected ball on cart path, but one-club placement could avoid path

Ruling: the side may still take obstruction relief from the selected ball before applying format placement. The opponent cannot require optional format placement first.

## 16.4 Scramble: one tee shot OB, one in play

Ruling: select the in-play ball. No penalty for the non-selected OB tee shot.

## 16.5 Scramble: both tee shots OB

Ruling: proceed under stroke-and-distance from the teeing area. Add one penalty stroke. The side is playing its third team stroke.

## 16.6 Scramble: selected ball in bunker footprint

Ruling: if no rake-and-place rule was announced, do not improve the bunker beyond what the Rules allow. If rake-and-place was announced, the side may smooth and place as allowed.

## 16.7 Scramble: first partner holes the putt

Ruling: the side's score is fixed. The partner's later putt cannot improve or change the score.

## 16.8 Scramble: partner plays from outside the placement area and hits a great shot

Ruling: that shot is not a valid selection. If the side selects it or plays forward from it, the side has played from a wrong place and may lose the hole.

## 16.9 Shamble: selected drive in rough, one club-length reaches fairway

Ruling: stay in rough if no Rules relief is taken.

## 16.10 Shamble: selected drive in rough with obstruction relief, legal relief area in fairway

Ruling: relief first. If free relief is available and the correct relief area is in fairway, both players may play second strokes from the legal fairway relief area.

## 16.11 Shamble: selected drive in penalty area, side wants relief

Ruling: each player who continues from the relief area adds the applicable penalty stroke to that player's individual score.

## 16.12 Shamble: player improves lie after second shot

Ruling: not allowed unless another Rule or Local Rule applies. After the second stroke, own-ball play is as it lies.

## 16.13 Shamble: partner stands behind teammate's putt to learn the line for own putt

Ruling: not allowed after own-ball phase begins. General penalty under Four-Ball principles.

## 16.14 Shamble: one partner holes out, other cannot improve side score

Ruling: the other partner may pick up in best-ball shamble match play unless a Committee rule requires holing out.

## 16.15 Best Ball: player with conceded putt continues to show line

Ruling: not allowed if continuing would help the partner. The conceded player's score stands, but the partner's score cannot count for the side on that hole.

## 16.16 Best Ball: one partner plays wrong ball

Ruling: penalize that player. The partner's score may still count unless the breach helped the partner or hurt an opponent.

## 16.17 Best Ball: one partner gets loss-of-hole penalty

Ruling: that partner has no score that can count for the side, but the other partner may continue and may still produce the side's score unless the penalty also applies to the partner or side.

## 16.18 Singles: player gives wrong stroke count

Ruling: if corrected in time, no loss of hole for wrong information. If not corrected in time and it affected the opponent's understanding or play, the player may lose the hole.

## 16.19 Singles or Best Ball: player plays out of turn

Ruling: no penalty, but the opponent may cancel the stroke promptly. If not cancelled promptly, the stroke counts.

## 16.20 Any format: players disagree about where a ball was

Ruling: use reasonable judgment and best available evidence. Evidence may include the marker, original lie, pitch mark, witness statements, cart path, sprinkler head, yardage marker, divot, video, and player testimony.

## 16.21 Any format: player knowingly ignores a penalty

Ruling: serious breach. Apply Rule 1.3 principles. Players may not agree to ignore a known Rule or known penalty.

## 16.22 Any format: local rule not announced

Ruling: do not apply an unannounced event-specific Local Rule. Course Local Rules already in effect may apply if they are part of the course rules.

## 16.23 Any format: ball moves after being placed in a relief area

Ruling: determine whether the movement was caused by the player, natural forces, opponent, partner, or outside influence. Apply the Rules for replacing or playing as it lies. Do not assume the ball can simply be re-placed without analyzing cause.

## 16.24 Any format: relief area seems worse than original lie

Ruling: if the player chooses or is required to take relief, the legal relief area controls even if worse. If free relief is optional, the player may choose not to take it and play as it lies, unless a no-play zone or other mandatory relief condition applies.

## 16.25 Any format: player wants relief from a boundary fence or OB stake

Ruling: no free relief from boundary objects unless a Local Rule says otherwise.

# 17. Decision Trees

## 17.1 Master decision tree

1. Is the question about the overall event total, pairings, standings, or team match points?
   - Yes: outside this guide; ask Committee.
   - No: continue.
2. Which format is being played?
   - Scramble, Shamble, Four-Ball, or Singles.
3. Is the format gross or net?
   - Scramble/Shamble: gross.
   - Four-Ball/Singles: net.
4. Which ball matters?
   - Scramble: selected ball.
   - Shamble: selected drive or own-ball ball.
   - Four-Ball/Singles: individual player's ball.
5. What is the issue?
   - Relief, penalty, wrong ball, wrong place, concession, order, advice, handicap stroke, or hole result.
6. Has a stroke already been made after the issue?
   - If yes, determine whether correction is still allowed.
7. Has the next hole started?
   - If yes, check whether the ruling request is still timely.
8. State ruling, procedure, penalty, affected player/side, and hole-score effect.

## 17.2 Relief decision tree

1. Is the ball the selected ball/drive or an individual own-ball?
2. What area is it in?
3. What condition is causing interference?
4. Is relief free, penalty, mandatory, or unavailable?
5. Determine reference point and relief area under the Rules.
6. For Scramble/Shamble selected ball/drive, let both partners play from the legal relief area under the format procedure.
7. For own-ball formats, the individual player proceeds under the Rules.
8. Add any penalty strokes to the correct player or side.

## 17.3 Scramble decision tree

1. Has the side selected a ball?
2. Is the selected ball valid?
3. Does the selected ball require or permit Rules relief?
4. If relief applies, take relief before format placement.
5. If no relief applies, place within the scramble placement area, same area and cut/condition.
6. Are both partners playing from a legal area?
7. Did any selected shot come from a wrong place?
8. Count one team stroke for each selected sequence plus side penalties.

## 17.4 Shamble decision tree

1. Has the side selected a drive?
2. Is the selected drive valid?
3. Does the selected drive require or permit Rules relief?
4. If relief applies, take relief before second-stroke placement.
5. Did each partner play a second stroke from the legal selected-drive area?
6. Once a player plays the second stroke, that player is in own-ball phase.
7. After own-ball phase begins, apply Four-Ball principles and no more shamble placement.
8. Use the lower gross partner score for the side unless another scoring method was announced.

## 17.5 Four-Ball decision tree

1. Identify which player's ball is involved.
2. Apply Rules to that player.
3. Determine whether the penalty affects only that player or also the partner/side.
4. Apply handicap strokes to each player's gross score.
5. Use the lower net partner score as the side score.
6. If one partner's score cannot count, check whether the other partner still has a valid score.

## 17.6 Singles decision tree

1. Apply the Rules to the player's own ball.
2. Add penalty strokes if required.
3. Apply handicap strokes for the hole.
4. Compare net scores.
5. Apply concessions, order-of-play rules, and wrong-information rules as needed.

# 18. In-Round Default Terms - Copy/Paste

Use this language if a short rules source is needed for the players or rules assistant:

All matches are match play. Scramble and Shamble are gross. Best Ball/Four-Ball and Singles are net. This guide applies only to in-round decisions and does not decide total team points, standings, pairings, or event tie-breaks.

In Scramble, both partners tee off, the side selects one valid ball, both partners play from the selected spot, and the process repeats until one valid team ball is holed or the hole is otherwise completed. Off the putting green, normal placement is within one club-length, no nearer the hole, same area and same cut/condition. On the putting green, normal placement is within one putter-head length, no nearer the hole. If the selected ball is entitled to Rules relief, relief is determined from the original selected ball position before format placement is applied.

In Shamble, both partners tee off, the side selects one valid drive, both players play second strokes from the selected-drive spot, and after those second strokes each player plays that player's own ball. The side's score is the lower gross partner score. Normal selected-drive placement is within one club-length, no nearer the hole, same area and same cut/condition. If the selected drive is entitled to Rules relief, relief is determined from the original selected-drive position before format placement is applied.

In Best Ball/Four-Ball, each player plays their own ball. The side's score is the lower net partner score. Four-Ball principles apply to partner order, advice, concessions, standing behind, wrong ball, and penalty allocation.

In Singles, each player plays their own ball. The lower net score wins the hole.

Relief-first rule: A side may not be forced to use scramble or shamble format placement before deciding whether official Rules relief applies. If the selected ball or selected drive has obstruction or abnormal-course-condition interference, determine the proper Rules relief area first. If that legal relief area is in a different cut, such as fairway instead of rough, the legal relief area controls.

Default penalty: the general penalty in match play is loss of hole. Penalty relief adds penalty stroke(s) to the correct player or side score and is not automatically loss of hole.

Disputes: do not play two balls in match play. State the issue, preserve a timely ruling request, and ask the Committee or referee if the sides cannot agree without ignoring a known Rule or penalty.
